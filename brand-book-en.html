01 — General Identity

Who is Antiqora Travel

Category: Luxury archaeological travel

Founding guide: Ipek Tarakçı — archaeologist

Operating languages: Italian · English · Turkish

Destinations: Egypt · Turkey · Italy

Group size: Maximum 10 people

Antiqora Travel is a luxury archaeological travel agency that leads exclusive groups (maximum 10 people) through Egypt, Turkey and Italy under the personal guidance of a professional archaeologist. It provides access to sites normally closed to the public, private sessions with local experts and itineraries built around each group's specific interests. This is not tourism: it is immersion.

02 — Brand Story

How Antiqora was born

Ipek Tarakçı is an archaeologist who has devoted her training and career to studying the ancient world. She has worked in the field across Turkey, Egypt and Italy — not as a tourist guide, but as a scholar who knows these sites from the inside: the areas not open to the public, the privileged access points, the colleagues in the field, the stories that appear in no caption.

Antiqora Travel was born out of that frustration — the realisation that most visitors to the world's great archaeological sites pass through them without ever truly understanding them. Queues, generic guides, twenty-minute stops in front of millennia-old ruins. Ipek wanted to build something entirely opposite.

The name — Antiqora: a fusion of antiquus (Latin, "ancient") and agorá (Greek, "square, meeting place"). A place where the past meets those willing to listen.

The model — Groups of maximum 10 people. No crowds, no megaphone, no standardised programme. Every journey begins with a conversation with the participants and is built to measure. Ipek leads every itinerary personally.

03 — Foundations

Core idea, promise, positioning

Core idea: History is lived, not watched. The world's great ancient sites only make sense when someone knows how to open them up for you. Antiqora Travel is that opening.

Brand promise: An experience that changes the way you understand the ancient world. Not a tourist visit, but an immersion guided by someone who has devoted their professional life to these places.

Positioning: Ultra-personalised luxury archaeological journeys, guided by authentic expertise, for those who refuse mass tourism and seek a culturally transformative experience. Not a tour operator. Not a generic premium travel agency. An archaeologist who opens her places to the right people.

08 — Tone of Voice

How we speak

Refined and cultured but never academic. Evocative — it conveys the weight of time, the silence of sites, the ancient dust. Precise — uses specific terms. Restrained — never one adjective more than necessary. Alive — history is not a catalogue.

Main Rules
  • Write little, with words that carry weight
  • No tourist catalogue language ever
  • Never say "unique" or "unforgettable" — show it
  • Use specific terms (not "ruins" but the name of the site)
  • Soft CTA: "Request your itinerary", not "Book now"
  • Structure: evocative detail → Antiqora difference → soft CTA

15 — Photography

Our visual world

Preferred subjects
  • Archaeological sites in warm light
  • Details of stone, sculpture, hieroglyphics
  • Ipek in the field — authentic, not posed
  • Small groups listening (never crowds)
  • Empty tomb interiors, theatres, temples
  • Desert or rocky landscapes at golden hour
Light & mood
  • Golden hour — warm, raking light
  • Sunset over sites — long shadows
  • Side light on carved stone
  • Dark interior lit by a single shaft of light
  • Mood: intimate · silent · exclusive · editorial
Always avoid
  • Generic stock photos of crowded tourist sites
  • Harsh midday light, oversaturation
  • Artificially posed subjects in front of ruins
  • Images that look like a travel agency catalogue
  • Heavy Instagram filters or cold colour grades

21 — Instagram Strategy

How to communicate

Instagram should work as a channel of discovery, cultural desire and contact requests — not a generic tourism showcase.

01
Expertise & Credibility

Ipek, archaeology, exclusive access, direct expertise. Make clear the difference between Antiqora and any other agency.

02
Places & Wonder

Specific sites, unseen details, precise historical facts. Educate and fascinate. Never "generic ruins" — always a specific place with a specific story.

03
Exclusivity & Intimacy

Small groups, privileged access, experience vs mass tourism. Make people desire what they cannot find elsewhere.

04
Soft Conversion

Itineraries, contact invitation, conversation prompt. Never aggressive CTA. "Start the conversation" not "Book now".

Recommended frequency: 3 reels/week · 2 carousels · Daily stories · 1 contact-oriented post every 5–6 days

Language: Main posts IT+EN (bilingual or alternating captions). Stories in IT. Reels with minimal text, understandable across all languages.

22 — Do & Don't

Operative rules

✓ Always do
  • Always refer to the guide as "archaeologist", not "guide"
  • Specify "max 10 people" or "groups of 10"
  • Use specific terms: site name, civilisation, historical period
  • Soft CTA: "Request your itinerary", "Start the conversation"
  • Photograph sites without crowds or at the right moment
  • Maintain visual consistency: gold on dark, always
  • Mention access to normally closed sites
  • Show why it's different — don't just say it
✗ Never do
  • Use "guided tour", "excursion", "transfer included"
  • Generic adjectives: unforgettable, breathtaking, one of a kind
  • Aggressive CTA: "Book now!", "Limited spots! Act fast!"
  • Stock photos with tourist crowds or generic glossy images
  • Inconsistent tone between website, IG and email
  • Travel agency catalogue language or standard tone
  • Explicitly compare against competitors
  • Posts without visual care or approximate copy
